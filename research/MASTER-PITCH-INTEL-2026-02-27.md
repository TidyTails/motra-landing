# ❓ HARD QUESTIONS & ANSWERS

## Q1: Why won't Waymo do this themselves?
**ANSWER:** ✅ DRAFTED
> "Three reasons:
>
> **1. They already outsource it.** Waymo uses Transdev with 1,000+ operators for fleet services today. They've made the build-vs-buy decision — they buy.
>
> **2. It's not their core competency.** Waymo's edge is autonomous driving software and safety. Fleet cleaning is operationally complex but not a differentiator. Every dollar they spend on cleaning infrastructure is a dollar not spent on AV tech.
>
> **3. The math doesn't work in-house.** At 400K rides/week across 10 cities, they'd need thousands of employees, facilities in every market, HR, training, benefits. A gig platform converts that fixed cost to variable cost — they pay per clean, not per employee.
>
> **Proof:** Uber just launched AV Services this week. If the big players thought they could do this better in-house, they wouldn't be building outsourced solutions."

## Q2: What's your moat?
**ANSWER:** ✅ DRAFTED
> "Our moat builds in three layers:
>
> **Layer 1 — First-Mover Network Effects (Year 1-2)**
> - First to build AV-specialized gig network in each city
> - Techs trained on AV-specific protocols (sensors, EVs, safety)
> - More techs → faster response times → happier fleet operators → more volume → more techs
> - Network effects are HYPERLOCAL (city-by-city) — requires heavy investment but creates defensibility
>
> **Layer 2 — Data Advantage (Year 2-3)**
> - Every service generates data: cleaning times, common issues, vehicle conditions
> - DoorDash has 30% of engineering in data roles — 2-3x industry average
> - Predictive maintenance insights become invaluable — switching cost increases
>
> **Layer 3 — Integration Lock-In (Year 3+)**
> - API integration with fleet management systems
> - Embedded in operator workflows
> - 5% improvement in retention = 25-95% profit increase (Bain)
>
> **The Uber analogy:** Uber's moat isn't the app — it's the network of drivers in every city."

## Q3: How do you get your first customer?
**ANSWER:** ✅ DRAFTED (DoorDash playbook)
> "We follow DoorDash's proven playbook: **Do things that don't scale.**
>
> **Phase 1: Manual Pilot (Month 1-3)**
> - Target: ONE fleet operator in ONE city (Phoenix or SF)
> - Warm intros via investors > cold outreach (3-5x better close rate)
> - Offer: Free pilot — 50 vehicles, 30 days
> - Delivery: Text-based dispatch, Google Sheets tracking — like DoorDash's Google Docs + Square + Find My Friends MVP
> - Be the operators ourselves to learn the product
>
> **Phase 2: Paid Contract (Month 4-6)**
> - Convert pilot to paid contract
> - Price 20% below Transdev cost
> - Lock in 6-month commitment
>
> **Phase 3: Launcher Expansion (Month 7-12)**
> - DoorDash's 6-8 week market launch process
> - Door-to-door engagement with fleet ops managers
> - Build app while revenue grows
>
> **Why this works:** We're not asking them to trust an app. We're asking them to try a service."

## Q7: What if Waymo acquires or builds this?
**ANSWER:** ✅ DRAFTED
> "That's actually a great outcome — **we get acquired.**
>
> But here's why they're more likely to partner than build:
>
> **1. They already outsource to Transdev**
> - If they wanted to build in-house, they would have. They chose to buy.
>
> **2. Acquisition is cheaper than building**
> - Fleet services acquisitions: $100M-$1.3B range (Zoox was $1.2B)
> - A $50-100M acquisition for a working network is cheaper than years of internal development
>
> **3. Multiple buyers = leverage**
> - Most likely acquirers: Waymo, Uber, Amazon (owns Zoox)
> - Competition for acquisition drives up price
>
> **Proof:** Waymo partners with TechForce, Amerit, Avis Budget Group, Moove, Terawatt, AND Transdev. They prefer partnerships."

> - "Gig workers are quitting Uber, DoorDash to find full-time jobs" — we're the upgrade
>
> **2. Competitive pay**
> - Transdev AV operators earn ~$58K/year
> - That's $28/hour — way above DoorDash
>
> **3. Sign-up bonuses**
> - Uber pays up to $2,760 for new drivers
> - We match or beat
>
> **4. Career progression (our edge)**
> - Transdev model: L1 Associate → L2 Operator → Supervisor → Manager

>
> **5. Referral bonuses**
> - Uber UK driver made $20K/month purely from referrals
> - We do $100-250 per successful referral
>
> **Worker acquisition cost benchmark:** $300-800 per active worker (gig), $5-10K (trucking)"
