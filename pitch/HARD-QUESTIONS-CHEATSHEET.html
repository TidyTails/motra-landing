MOTRA — Hard Questions Cheat Sheet

BUSINESS MODEL

Q: Why won't Waymo just do this themselves?

1. They already outsource. Waymo uses Transdev, Moove, Avis, TechForce, Amerit, Terawatt today. Build vs. buy = they buy.

2. Not core competency. Waymo's edge is AI. Every $ on cleaning = $ not on autonomy.

3. Math doesn't work. 20+ cities = thousands of employees. Gig model = variable cost.

Q: What's your moat?

Year 1-2: Network effects — first AV gig network per city. Hyperlocal.

Year 2-3: Data advantage — predictive maintenance from service data.

Year 3+: Integration lock-in — API embedded in fleet systems.

Q: How do you get first customer?

Phase 1: Manual pilot — ONE operator, ONE city. Free 30-day, 50 vehicles. Text dispatch.

Phase 2: Convert to paid. Price 20% below Transdev.

Phase 3: Use reference to expand. Target $100K MRR by month 12.

Q: What if AV market slows?

Even if robotaxis slow, 250K+ commercial EVs need service:

• Amazon: 30K → 100K EVs

• FedEx: 200K+ vehicles

• UPS: 18K+ alt-fuel

COMPETITIVE

Q: Uber just launched AV Services. Threat?

They validated our market. But Uber is a competitor to Waymo. Waymo won't use Uber infrastructure.

MOTRA is Switzerland — neutral platform serving everyone.

Q: What about Tesla cleaning robots?

Patents only — years away. Relies on Optimus (still demo stage). And Tesla-only.

Q: What if Waymo acquires you?

Great outcome for investors.

Waymo = $126B. A $200M acquisition is a rounding error.

Multiple buyers (Waymo, Uber, Amazon) = competition drives price up.
