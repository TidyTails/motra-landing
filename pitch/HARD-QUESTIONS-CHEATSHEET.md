# MOTRA — Hard Questions Cheat Sheet
## For Adonis — Investor Meeting Prep

---

## BUSINESS MODEL QUESTIONS

### Q: Why won't Waymo just do this themselves?
**ANSWER:**
> "Three reasons. First, they already outsource it — Waymo uses Transdev, Moove, Avis, TechForce, Amerit, and Terawatt today. The build vs. buy decision has been made: they buy.
>
> Second, it's not their core competency. Waymo's edge is autonomous driving software. Every dollar spent on cleaning infrastructure is a dollar not spent on AI.
>
> Third, the math doesn't work in-house. 20+ cities means thousands of employees, facilities, HR, training, benefits. Our gig model converts fixed cost to variable cost — they pay per clean, not per employee.
>
> Proof: Uber just launched AV Services this week. If big players thought they could do this better internally, they wouldn't be building outsourced solutions."

---

### Q: What's your moat? What stops someone from copying this?
**ANSWER:**
> "Our moat builds in three layers.
>
> Year 1-2: Network effects. We're building the first AV-specialized gig network city by city. Network effects are hyperlocal — more techs means faster response, which means happier operators, which means more volume, which attracts more techs.
>
> Year 2-3: Data advantage. Every service generates data on cleaning times, common issues, vehicle conditions. DoorDash has 30% of their engineering in data roles for a reason — this data becomes a competitive moat.
>
> Year 3+: Integration lock-in. API integration with fleet management systems embeds us in operator workflows. Bain found that 5% improvement in retention drives 25-95% profit increase. Switching costs become real."

---

### Q: How do you get your first customer?
**ANSWER:**
> "We follow DoorDash's playbook: do things that don't scale.
>
> Phase 1 is a manual pilot — one operator, one city, probably Phoenix or SF. We offer a free 30-day pilot with 50 vehicles. Text-based dispatch, Google Sheets tracking. We ARE the operators ourselves to learn the product.
>
> Phase 2, we convert to a paid contract. Price 20% below what they're paying Transdev. Lock in a 6-month commitment.
>
> Phase 3, we use that reference customer to expand. Add a second city, launch the app, target $100K MRR by month 12.
>
> We're not asking them to trust an app. We're asking them to try a service. Warm intros through our investor network get us in the door — that's 3-5x better close rate than cold outreach."

---

### Q: What if the AV market slows down?
**ANSWER:**
> "Even if robotaxi growth hits regulatory delays or technical hurdles, we have adjacent markets.
>
> Amazon has 30,000 Rivian EVs today, targeting 100,000 by 2030. FedEx has 200,000+ vehicles transitioning to electric. UPS has 18,000+ and 10,000 more on order.
>
> That's 250,000+ commercial EVs that need regular cleaning and maintenance regardless of what happens with robotaxis.
>
> Robotaxis are our beachhead — the fastest-growing, highest-frequency use case. Commercial EV fleets are the expansion play."

## COMPETITIVE QUESTIONS

### Q: Uber just launched AV Services. Aren't they a threat?
**ANSWER:**
> "Actually, they just validated our market. But here's the thing — Uber is a competitor to Waymo. Waymo will never use Uber's infrastructure to service their fleet.
>
> Same with Zoox, which Amazon owns. They won't use Uber either.
>
> MOTRA is Switzerland. We're a neutral platform that can serve Waymo, Zoox, Cruise, everyone — without conflict of interest.
>
> Also, Uber's focus is depot-based. We're mobile-first. Different model."

---

### Q: What about Tesla's cleaning robots?
**ANSWER:**
> "Tesla has patents for self-cleaning robots, but they're years away from deployment. They rely on Optimus, which is still in demo stage.
>
> More importantly, Tesla's solution only works for Tesla vehicles. Waymo, Zoox, Cruise still need human labor.
>
> By the time Tesla's robots are ready, we'll have the network, the data, and the customer relationships."

---

### Q: What if Waymo just acquires you?
**ANSWER:**
> "That's actually a great outcome for investors.
>
> Waymo is valued at $126 billion. A $100-200 million acquisition to lock in fleet services is a rounding error for them.
>
> And we have multiple potential acquirers — Waymo, Uber, Amazon. Competition for acquisition drives up price.
>
> Our goal is to build enough value that acquisition is inevitable and attractive."
